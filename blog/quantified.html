quantified

Is measurement necessary for improvement? Further, is the process of measuement alone sufficient to make improvements? Having recently read some books that make this claim, I was inspired to try it for myself. I decided to just start measuring things and see what would happen, without necessarily having a specific goal in mind.
After a two month hiatus after getting surgery, I had a renewed focus on improving my general health. I first started to wear a heart rate monitor (on my garmin) to keep track of daily activity. All of a sudden, I started ditching my usual commute on the subway to a commute on a citibike, because I liked what that did for my numbers.
Second, as I started running again, I started uploading my workouts to strava. This also made me want to go out much more often, even if the posts were private and nobody could see them except me. I started slowly pushing the maximum distance of my long runs, and posting them gave me a renewed sense of inspiration each week. I mentally committed to the "no two days off" rule and have stuck to it since.
On the road to get monthly blood tests this year, I also started focusing more on key biomarkers that I could adjust through my diet. Remembering to take supplements was easier when I knew I would be able to actually see that they were working in a few weeks.
I also started tracking my weight. It's not my goal to lose a significant amount of weight, but I did want to get an idea of how training was changing my diet.
One key thing to remember is that absolute values in the measurements are not really significant. Most measuement devices available to consumers have a high degree of inaccuracy. For example, the scale at home and at my doctor may read out my weight as over 10 pounds apart, and that does not mean I gained or lost any weight. However, when measuring with the same inaccurate device over and over, a trend line can be observed. More important than the absolute number is the trend, and this directional line is most important.
Directionally, I am making improvements. I am taking this all much slower than I have done in past efforts to improve my health. I have a longer horizon in mind for when to expect results. I am not sure if this extra patience is an accidental effect of just getting older or actual improvement from my efforts, but I'll take it.
So far, the simple tactic of "just measure" has worked. Without a specific goal in mind but rather a directional trend, I feel less pressure to achieve something quickly, which I hope will contribute to the formation of long term (perhaps even lifelong) habits. As long as I am steering myself in the right direction, I will get the results I want naturally, without much effort.
